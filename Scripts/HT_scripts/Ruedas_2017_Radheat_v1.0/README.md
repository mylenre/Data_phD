# radheat (v.1.0, 18/10/2017)
This program calculates the energy available as heat or for chemical reactions and the specific heat production of six nuclides (<sup>26</sup>Al, <sup>40</sup>K, <sup>60</sup>Fe, <sup>232</sup>Th, <sup>235</sup>U, and <sup>238</sup>U), which are geologically important as heat sources, especially in the context of the formation, evolution, and geodynamics of terrestrial planetary bodies.

Reference:

Ruedas, T. (2017): Radioactive heat production of six geologically important nuclides. Geochem. Geophys. Geosys., 18(9), 3530-3541, [doi:10.1002/2017GC006997](http://dx.doi.org/10.1002/2017GC006997).
